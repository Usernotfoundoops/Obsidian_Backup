#### 2.1.1 Crystallization of Solids

| Crystalline                                                                                             | Amorphous                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Regular arrangement of atoms (ordered solids)                                                           | No long-range order (disordered)                                                                                                                                                                              |
| They are in the absolute minima of their potential energy i.e. at the thermodynamically favorable state | They are usually in the local minima of their potential energy i.e. in a meta stable state unable to exit this minima due to low temperature and thermally unattainable potential barrier has to be overcome. |
| Translation invariance                                                                                  | No translational invariance                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| NaCl, Diamond.                                                                                          | Polymer, Glass, Amorphous semiconductors, random alloys.                                                                                                                                                      |

#### 2.1.2 Crystal System, Lattice, and Structure
 

1. Lattice Points can be given by $R = \sum_{i=1}^d n_i a_i$ (d = dimension of the lattice space, a = basis vectors) 
2.  Unit Cell
	- Primitive Unit Cell - contains only 1 lattice point
	- Conventional Unit Cell - more than 1 lattice points but *orthogonal*
	- Wigner-Seitz Cell - points closer to that specific LP
3. [[MATH Area vector.excalidraw|Area]] **$A = |a_1 \times a_2|$**
4. Volume(scalar triple product) **$V = |a_1 \cdot (a_2 \times a_3)|$**
5. [[Dimension 2 Crystal Systems.canvas|2 Dimensional Crystal Configurations/Symmetries]]
6.  The description of objects in the unit cell with respect to a reference lattice point in the unit cell constitutes a *basis*.
