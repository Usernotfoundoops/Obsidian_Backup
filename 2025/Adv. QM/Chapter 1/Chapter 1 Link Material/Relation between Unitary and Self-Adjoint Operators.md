

### 1. Function of a self-adjoint operator
For $A = A^\dagger$ (self-adjoint), applying adjoint to a function just conjugates it: $f(A)^\dagger = \bar{f}(A)$

Two functions with $A$ multiply as: $f_1(A)f_2(A) = (f_1 \times f_2)(A)$

---
### 2. Choosing $f(x) = e^{ikx}$
$$\implies (e^{ikA})^\dagger = e^{-ikA}$$

Set $f_1 = e^{ikx}$ and $f_2 = \bar{f} = e^{-ikx}$:
$$e^{ikA}e^{-ikA} = 1(A) = \mathbb{1}_\mathcal{H}$$

So $e^{ikA}$ is **unitary**.

---
### 3. Unitarity condition confirmed ($UU^\dagger = \mathbb{1}$)
- Evolution is **reversible**
- Inner products are **preserved**
- Total probability **stays 1**

---
### 4. One Parameter Unitary Group (1PUG)
Instead of applying two unitaries separately, add parameters and apply once,
$$U_k U_{k'} = U_{k+k'}$$
$$\lim_{\epsilon \to 0} U_{\epsilon+k}\psi = U_k\psi \quad \forall\psi \in \mathcal{H}$$
---
### 5. Stone's Theorem
Every 1PUG is of the form $U_k = e^{ikA}$ for some self-adjoint $A$. We recover $A$ with:
$$A\psi = -i\frac{d}{dk}\bigg|_{k=0} U_k\psi$$

| Self-adjoint $A$ | Unitary $U_k = e^{ikA}$ |
| ---------------- | ----------------------- |
| Observable       | Symmetry/evolution      |
| Hermitian        | Unitary group           |
| Generates $U_k$  | Generated by $A$        |
|                  |                         |

---
### Examples
**Time evolution** via Hamiltonian $H$:
$$U_t = U(t,t_0) = e^{-iH\frac{t-t_0}{\hbar}}$$

**Spatial translation** by $\vec{\delta}$:
$$(T_{\vec{\delta}}\psi)(\vec{x}) = \psi(\vec{x} - \vec{\delta})$$
$$T_{\vec{\delta}} = e^{-\frac{i}{\hbar}\vec{\delta}\cdot\vec{p}}$$

---
### One line summary
> Self-adjoint operators are the **generators** of symmetries. Unitary operators are the **symmetries** themselves. Stone's theorem says they are in perfect 1-1 correspondence.
