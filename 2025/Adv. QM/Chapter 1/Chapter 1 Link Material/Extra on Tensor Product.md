1. Two Distinguishable Spinless Particle
$$\mathcal{H} = L^2(\mathbb{R}^3, d^3x) \otimes L^2(\mathbb{R}^3, d^3x)$$
	Product State Representation:
	$$(\psi_1(\vec{x}_1), \psi_2(\vec{x}_2)) \equiv \psi_1 \otimes \psi_2 \quad \text{with} \quad \psi_1(\vec{x}_1) \times \psi_2(\vec{x}_2)$$
	Entangled State Representation:
	$$\psi(\vec{x}_1, \vec{x}_2) = \sum_{ij} c_{ij}\psi_i(\vec{x}_1)\psi_j'(\vec{x}_2)$$
	Both of them can be represented using:
	$$\mathcal{H} = L^2(\mathbb{R}^6, d^3x_1 d^3x_2)$$
	It says how the Hilbert Space contains all the configuration of states for these two particles. so $L^2(\mathbb{R}^3, d^3x) \otimes L^2(\mathbb{R}^3, d^3x) = L^2(\mathbb{R}^6, d^3x_1 d^3x_2)$ always

2. Non-Relativistic Electron coupled with its Spin:
	$H = L^2(R^3,d^3x)\otimes C^2$ ONE POSITIONAL INFO COUPLED WITH SPIN
	$H = L^2(R^3,d^3x)\otimes (C\oplus C)$
	$H = (L^2(R^3,d^3x)\otimes C)\oplus (L^2(R^3,d^3x)\otimes C)$ 
	$H = L^2(R^3,d^3x)\oplus L^2(R^3,d^3x)$ TWO POSITIONAL INFO FOR DIFFERENT SPINS OF ONE ELECTRON
